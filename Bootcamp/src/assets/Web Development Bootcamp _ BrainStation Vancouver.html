What You'll Learn

  • HTML5 HTML training to help you master the internet’s foundational language.
  • CSS3 Use CSS3 to make your website standout by customizing your layout, appearance, and adding animations.
  • Bootstrap Apply this modern front-end framework to easily create responsive sites on mobile, tablet and desktop.
  • SASS & BEM Learn industry best practices for styling websites with CSS tools and methodologies.
  • JavaScript Learn to program with the language of the web, using the latest ECMAScript6 standard.
  • JQuery Use one of the web’s most popular libraries, jQuery, to add interactivity, animations, and dynamic changes to your webpage.
  • NodeJS Dive deeper into JavaScript programming by creating NodeJS programs such as web scrapers.
  • ExpressJS Learn how to create back-end web apps using ExpressJS, a NodeJS web application framework.
  • ReactJS Create front-end applications with Facebook’s widely used JavaScript library for building user interfaces.
  • Relational Databases Manage your web applications data using commonly used relational databases.
  • RESTful APIs Learn how to use third-party APIs and how to build your own RESTful APIs to manage your app’s data needs.
  • Model-View-Controller (MVC) Learn the basics of MVC architecture by creating server-side rendered web applications using ExpressJS.
  • Component-based Architecture Create front-end apps in a component-based architecture using ReactJS and flux-esque state management libraries.
  • Git Practice how to remotely manage your code and collaborate with developers in a team environment using industry software.
  • NPM Access the world’s largest open source package management system to rapidly build new apps.
  • Agile Software Development Learn the basics of the iterative approach to software development through group projects.
  • Authentication & Authorization Integrate password encryption and user authentication with libraries such as Bcrypt and JWT.

The Experience

BrainStation's full-time Web Development program is tailored to those looking to turn their idea into a startup, make a career switch into technology, or drive their business with digital. It's a challenging yet fun and engaging learning experience that emphasizes collaboration.

By the end of the 10 weeks, you'll have acquired the skills needed to become a Junior Web Developer, as well as a network of like-minded individuals and industry mentors; relationships you'll continue to develop beyond the classroom.

“BrainStation allowed me to learn at my own pace, but guided me whenever I needed help. The best part about the teaching style was that they never just gave me the answer, but always asked me questions or gave me hints. Now when I run into problems, I know I can find the answers myself.”

Jackson

Web Development Alumni

How You'll Learn

Delivery

Unlike most classroom experiences, the full-time Web Development program focuses on hands-on, project-based learning. BrainStation content is delivered through our own Learning Portal, meant to optimize the speed at which new topics and concepts are introduced.

Adaptive Curriculum

You’ll learn content that is carefully curated based on what is being used day-to-day by Vancouver’s best Designers. New tools and resources are constantly being introduced, and as a full-time student you’ll be the first to know.

Become a Lifelong Learner

One of the main program objectives is to teach you how to be a lifelong learner; this means going beyond teaching you industry-leading tools and languages, but teaching you how to be resourceful. It’s essential that you ‘learn how to learn’ so you can continue to upgrade your skills post-graduation.

“You will not only learn hands-on skills in your respective field of study, but also learn how to learn. Our educational delivery model reflects this; you will be pushed to challenge your own assumptions and adopt new mindsets on the regular. This iterative approach to learning is indeed intense, and is only possible thanks to the extensive support you will receive from our talented Educators who are also expert lifelong learners themselves.”

Jin - Sr. Manager, Full Time Programs

The Learning Environment

Our Vancouver campus is located in the heart of downtown at the corner of Granville and Pender, steps away from Waterfront Station and in the same neighborhood as some of the most influential technology companies including Microsoft, Amazon, Shopify and more. The new 20,000 sq ft Vancouver campus opened in 2017 and is a learning environment unlike any other. Grab your morning coffee at our very own Quantum café, and enjoy learning in a state-of-the-art classroom. Hang out in our bright, loft-style lounge and unwind with a game of ping pong or head to the student lounge to work on your final project.

As a BrainStation student, you’ll also have the opportunity to stick around after project time and attend a BrainStation evening event, whether it be an inspiring Panel Discussion, community social, hands-on workshop, Speaker Series or Fireside Chat. Full-time students receive learning credits to go towards evening events, and it’s a great opportunity to network and mingle with the local tech and startup community.

Admissions Process

BrainStation selects only committed individuals who will thrive in our fast-paced, collaborative program. Throughout the application process, you will be challenged to learn new concepts through self-guided assessments and critically assess your readiness through an interview with our Success Team. To begin your application, please request a Course Information Package on the course page and complete the short online application to get in touch with our Admissions team. The Course Information Package will outline next stages in applying for the program.

We recommend that all individuals begin their application process one month prior to their intended start date to ensure sufficient time to complete all stages of the admissions process, including mandatory prep assessments.

  • 1

    Get In Touch

    Request additional information for the course and schedule a call with a learning advisor.

  • 2

    Apply and Prepare

    Begin the admissions process and get access to the prep course.

  • 3

    Complete Interview

    Upon completion of the prep course conduct your admissions interview.

  • Acceptance

    If successful, sign your enrollment contract and receive your acceptance letter.

Applicants who do not successfully pass the admissions interview will have the opportunity to re-apply for the next cohort
